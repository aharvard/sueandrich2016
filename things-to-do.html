Things to Do

Explore Atlanta

We welcome guests to explore Atlanta during their visit! Check out this map of our favorite destinations and places to eat and drink or see the list below.

Our top 10 sights and activities

High Museum of Art Atlanta's most renowned museum features world-class exhibitions in the heart of Atlanta's Midtown neighborhood.

Center for Civil and Human Rights This is the newest museum in town, and has powerful exhibitions about the American Civil Rights Movement and global human rights movements, with a gallery featuring the papers of Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.

Piedmont Park Often considered one of Atlanta’s most scenic parks, you can take in views of Atlanta’s prime green space and the iconic skyline at the same time. The park was re-designed by the sons of Frederick Law Olmstead—the famous designer of New York City’s Central Park.

Ponce City Market The newly-renovated PCM has been the source of much anticipation in town. This old Sears, Roebuck & Company building has been repurposed into a multi-use market, with an impressive food hall and array of retail stores.

Jimmy Carter Presidential Library The state's only Presidential Library, the Carter Center and Presidential Library includes comprehensive exhibits on Carter’s time in office as ell as on his public health legacy.

Georgia Aquarium The largest aquarium in the Western Hemisphere, the Georgia Aquarium has 10 million gallons of fresh and marine water and tens of thousands of animals. You can even pet a stingray!

Martin Luther King, Jr. National Historic Site With a visitor’s center, museum, and historic buildings like the Ebenezer Baptist Church and Dr. King’s birth home, this site is a must-see.

Atlanta Botanical Gardens This impressive botanical garden contains 30 acres of plant collections for display, education, research, conservation, and enjoyment. Accessible via Piedmont Park.

Historic Oakland Cemetery We encourage you to explore this beautiful Victorian-style cemetery before our wedding ceremony! Oakland Cemetery tells the history of Atlanta through those buried there—including many of the city’s prominent figures.

CNN Center The world headquarters of CNN are located in Atlanta, and the studio tours offer an interesting glimpse of news and broadcasting in action.
